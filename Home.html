Connectivity

Delivery of patient care has been enhanced by connecting medical devices. However, increased connectivity puts strain on hospital networks, which are often blighted by legacy infrastructure. Cyber risk and complexity are increased unless software is kept up-to-date and components are compatible.

Regulation

Increased cyber risk, coupled with the proliferation of software and AI have brought about imminent changes to the regulatory framework for medical devices. Hospitals will need to adapt their existing practices as well as manage the priorities from IT/network and governance teams that now also have a stake in the management of device safety.

Complexity and Silos

Once upon a time, we had patients, clinical teams, medical devices with dedicated medical engineers to manage them.
In today's connected world, medical teams access patient data in the cloud. Virtual wards, software and AI as a medical device have to be integrated alongside their supply chain. In order for data to move through the network and deliver timely results, multiple teams and data silos are now involved; all critical to patient care. This linkage requires effective communication which, in itself, introduces its own set of challenges.

Our solution is build from scratch with the needs of Medical Devices now and in the near future in mind. Flexibility is provided using a number of modules

Facilitating greater collaboration between teams that have remained siloed to date is a key element of our solution.
