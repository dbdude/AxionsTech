“MHRA have almost 2 million different products registered for use in the UK, of which we estimate that around 500,000 different product types are regularly used in the NHS. Most individual Trusts will use around 30,000 different products – this is a tremendous number of products to navigate.”

Policy paper - Medical technology strategy Published 3 February 2023

Ageing populations, the rise in chronic illnesses and infectious diseases, the shift towards early diagnostics and preventive medicine are increasing demand for medical devices.

The Covid-19 pandemic ushered along major change in the adoption of virtual wards, wearables and telemedicine.

The industry is undergoing a technological revolution with 3D Printing, AI/ML, 5G, Medical robotics, Genomic medicine so the very definition of a medical device is changing at breakneck speed and requirements to keep patients safe and deliver efficient and effective care need to adapt in tandem.

In August 2022, a ransomware attack on an IT supplier to the NHS caused widespread outages affecting services including patient referrals, ambulance dispatch, out-of-hours appointment bookings, mental health services and emergency prescriptions. Some services were forced to resort to pen and paper.

Statistics show that 30% of all large data breaches occur in hospitals

In 2021 over 40 million patient records were compromised in the USA, rising to 50 million in 2022. 60% of healthcare data breaches in 2021 were reportedly caused by third-party vendors.
